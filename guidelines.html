THE HARDEST IMPOSSIBLE LEVELS LIST RULES

Owned by KrX, Vaixen and Furii
Join us on Discord to submit levels: https://discord.gg/MHx4Nbp

    I | PLACEMENT GUIDELINES

  1. At the time of their creation, levels must be considered harder than the current hardest legitimately verified rated level on the list.
  2. The difficulty of a two-player level will be judged on their difficulty to complete solo.
  3. Levels cannot be shorter than 30 seconds in the editor.
  4. A level needs to pass quality control to be placed on the list.
  5. Revotes on levels are prohibited, unless the level gets major updates like deco changes, buffs that would move it at least 50 spots or extensions.
  6. If a level that’s harder than the current hardest rated level is verified, it skips quality control.
  7. If a level is found to be in violation of any of the List’s rules, it will be Marked for Removal. Levels that are Marked for Removal will have their entries on the List highlighted, and an explanation will be provided in the “Marked for Removal” section of the List. If the outstanding violations are not resolved 3 days after being Marked, the level will be removed from the List.
    <1> Classic levels will be placed based on their intended routes. Platformer placements will correspond to their easiest routes, including those with skips.

    II | QUALITY STANDARDS

  1. The quality of a level’s gameplay and decoration will be judged by the quality control members on a case-by-case basis through a vote. A level needs 50% or more positive votes to pass and be added to the list. Some general guidelines used when judging the quality of a level are:
    1. Levels determined to be low effort and/or created for the sole purpose of a list placement and considered to not have any distinctive or creative quality, will likely not be added to the list.
    2. Levels 30 to 59 seconds long may be judged more harshly compared to longer levels.
    3. Levels highly relying on a popular gimmick, such as the “Nine Circles” style, should have some semblance of creativity or quality not found in the original level.
    4. A level can have “old-style” decoration, but the decoration and gameplay should be suitable for the time period in which it is intended.
      1. Decoration quality standards increase the older the intended version is. Old style levels should have some semblance of creativity, quality of execution or uniqueness that sets them apart.
      2. Exceptions to limited feature usage can be made, mostly in cases where it is tedious or untenable to use said features, as long as they remain within the purview of update capabilities. The following items (in this context) are generally permissible: layering, corner blocks, slabs, LBG, Player Color 1/2 and the usage of pulse triggers to replace two colour triggers in rapid succession.
    5. Levels in the List’s Top 10 may be judged with harsher standards.
    6. If a level is a remake or buffed version of an existing level, it should be buffed in a transformative way and or have new or revamped decoration (e.g. Silent Clubstep by Maus999).
    While the above rules specifically are not hard set, they play a major part in the assessments of levels. They do not need to follow all of these, as long as they are deemed to be of overall good quality.
  2. A level must be finished according to the creator to be placed on the List unless one of the following is true:
    1. The level was never finished and/or is considered to be forever unfinished (Battle of the Shades [Old Version] by GD Virus)
    2. The differences between the unfinished and finished have significant parts that are different enough in gameplay and decoration (e.g. Yatagarasu [Old Version] by ViPriN)
    3. The version is and unnerfed version or a version before the level underwent balancing by the final verifier (e.g. Unnerfed VR by KrampuX)
    It’s important to note that these rules will mostly apply to levels more known in the overall community. Every level will still go through quality control.
  3. If a level is significant to the List or impossible level history, exemptions from the Decoration and Gameplay Quality Standards rules can be made. The significance of each level will be judged on a case-by-case basis.
  4. If a list-eligible level (in its most current legitimately verified state) is or used to be rated, the level may be placed without regard to any of our quality standards / procedures.

    III | PHYSICAL POSSIBILITY & OTHER LIMITATIONS

  1. Levels must be physically possible on at least one game-version and passable from beginning to end in normal mode on (at least) one frame-rate, to the exclusion of any external modifiers apart from FPS bypass.
    1. Changing the frame-rate during gameplay is not allowed.
    2. All levels that are submitted to the List require their intended frame rate to be publicly stated. If an easier frame rate is later found it will replace the previous frame rate on the list. A level cannot require a frame-rate below 1.
    3. If a level is verified, its listed frame-rate will be changed to the value used during verification.
  2. In the spirit of competition, triggers which count frames with the intent to force frame perfects are disallowed. This is due to the ease of creating extreme difficulty and overall low effort required in both playtesting and creating.
  3. Timings that simulate randomness are allowed in levels. However, the level’s placement will be based on optimal luck.

    IV | SPAM SPEED LIMIT

  1. Classic:
    1. Levels may not include sections where clicks per player must exceed:
      1. 15 clicks in a second
      2. 3 clicks in a frame
      3. A rate of 45 cps in any stint of 5 or more clicks.
    2. In two-player mode levels, both players must separately follow these rules.
  2. Platformer
    1. Platformers may not include sections where jump-interaction (JI) inputs per player must exceed:
      1. 15 in a second
      2. 3 in a frame
      3. A rate of 45 inputs per second in any stint of 5 or more inputs.
    2. Additionally—for platformer—the amount of non-JI inputs (e.g. left, right) that may be performed in a second is limited to 20 in total, and 15 per input type. Rules b. and c. apply
    3. In two-player mode levels, both players must separately follow these rules.
    3. <2> If, in Classic mode, you are using an event trigger to access inputs that would otherwise be unavailable, you must follow Platformer rules.

    V | ALTERNATIVE VERSIONS

  1. If a level is made impossible by bugs or changes introduced in one or another update (e.g. Asterisk by AuraXalaiv), the level may be added with an asterisk so long as it remains physically possible in the update it was created in.
  2. An asterisk may also be used if the only available copies of a level require a fix to be played as intended (e.g. forced secret ways, auto versions, bugged copies).
  3. If a level is uploaded with the intent to be public by its creator and is later deleted, overwritten, or otherwise lost, accurate reuploads will be credited (e.g. Silent clubstep by TheRealSailent, uploaded by Compendium). Levels that are uploaded but do not fit this criteria (leaks) will be marked as Unreleased and not credited.
    1. If a creator specifically wants their levels or versions of their levels to be excluded from the List, they will be removed and no copy will be credited.
  4. If a level is fixed to fit the rules of the list, it can be added and marked as uploaded by the person who fixed the level (e.g. Silent Circles by TheRealSailent and Cyrillic, uploaded by 860).
      If the original level qualifies for historical significance exemptions, only one fix will also qualify.

    VI | WORLD RECORDS

  1. World records can currently only be submitted through the #wr-submissions channel in the Impossible Levels List Discord Server, when submitting please follow the pinned template.
    1. When submitting a world record for the world record competition include the date the world record was achieved. World records submitted long after the original date of their achievement will be disqualified from the competition but will be accepted on the list.
  2. World records must be attained legitimately by the standard of the Pointercrate Demon List; platformer records are held to the standard of the Platformer List.
    1. You may bypass to any frame rate greater than or equal to 1.
    2. World records using the noclip accuracy mod are allowed, as long as the accuracy upon completion is 100%
  3. World records must not be achieved on “bug-fixed” or in any other way altered versions of a level
  4. Verifications or first completions of a level count as world records.

    5. CLASSIC:

  5. Levels without records have a stated minimum progress percentage.
  6. World records must closely follow the intended path, secret ways do not count.
  7. Levels on the main list have their world records counted in increments of 0.1%, and levels on the extended list in increments of 1%.
    1. Some levels may have a lower percentage requirement depending on their nature.
    2. Only the first player to attain a tied world record will be added to the List.
  8. If your record was achieved using the “click between frames” mod, it may be accepted but will be marked in case this rule gets overwritten.

    9. PLATFORMER:

  9. Progress is measured based on the amount of distance covered in relation to the level’s endpoint. World records will be judged (on these terms) on a case by case basis.
  10. In the case that a run is tied in distance, the faster run will take the world-record.

    11. PLATFORMER TAS:

  11. For a world record TAS to be accepted, you must achieve a lower in-game time than the current record-holder.
  12. Skips are allowed; if the creator does not want a certain skip in their level, they will need to fix it themselves.
  13. Your physics frame rate must be between 60 and 240.
